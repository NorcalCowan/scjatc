Emergency Procedures

Evacuation

Primary (on-site) Location

  1. Initiate evacuation announcement
  2. Notify appropriate first responders
  3. Acquire JATC Response Tool Box and student rosters
  4. Using evacuation routes, escort building occupants to assembly location
  5. Take attendance and report to site administrator
  6. Remain in assembly area until further instructions are received
  7. Do not re-enter building until "All Clear" is provided
  8. Take additional action as needed

Secondary (off-site) Location

  1. Initiate off-site evacuation announcement
  2. Notify appropriate first responders
  3. Acquire JATC Response Tool Box and student rosters
  4. Using evacuation routes, escort building occupants to assembly location
  5. Take attendance and prior to leaving the site
  6. Proceed to secondary (off-site) location
  7. Take attendance and report to site administrator
  8. Remain in assembly area until further instructions are received
  9. Do not re-enter building until "All Clear" is provided

Evacuation – On-site

This action provides for the orderly movement of students and staff along prescribed routes from inside the facility to a designated outside area of safety. Evacuation is considered appropriate for, but is not limited to, the following events:

  • Fire
  • Post-earthquake
  • Post-lockdown
  • Bomb threat
  • Gas leak
Announcement

An announcement should be made over the public address system and by staff in classrooms. This announcement should be similar to the following:

Attention please. We need to institute an EVACUATION of the building. Staff is to take their students to their designated Assembly Area. Students please remain with your Instructor.

Assisting Those With Disabilities

The needs and preferences of non-ambulatory individuals will vary. Those at ground floor locations may be able to exit without help. Others may have minimal ability to move and lifting may be dangerous. Some non-ambulatory people also have respiratory complications. Remove them from smoke and vapors immediately.

Visually-impaired
  • Announce the type of emergency
  • Offer your arm for guidance
  • Tell person where you are going, obstacles you encounter
  • When you reach safety, ask if further help is needed
Hearing-impaired
  • Turn lights on/off to gain person’s attention -OR-
  • Indicate directions with gestures –OR-
  • Write a note with evacuation directions
Crutches, Canes, or Walkers
  • Evacuate these individuals as injured persons
  • Assist and accompany to evacuation site, if possible -OR-
  • Use a sturdy chair (or one with wheels) to move person -OR-
  • Help carry individual to safety
Wheelchairs
  • Give priority assistance to wheelchair users with electrical respirators
  • If wheelchair is too heavy to take downstairs, determine the best carry options
  • Reunite person with the wheelchair as soon as it is safe to do so
  • Use evacuation chair if available and needed
Evacuation – Off-site

Off-site evacuation is implemented when it is unsafe to remain on the site and evacuation to an off-site assembly area is required. This action provides for the orderly movement of students and staff along prescribed routes from inside the buildings to a designated area of safety. Off-site evacuation is considered appropriate for, but is not limited to, the following events:

  • Fire
  • Post-earthquake
  • Post-lockdown
  • Bomb threat
  • Gas leak

Fire

Inside Building

  1. Activate fire alarm / emergency communications system
  2. Evacuate building
  3. Shut off gas to building
  4. Prevent re-entry into building
  5. Evaluate the situation
  6. Take additional action as needed

Outside / Adjacent Building

  1. Determine if evacuation is necessary
  2. If necessary, evacuate to secondary (off-site) location
  3. Ensure all occupants have left the building
  4. Post a notice stating "Building closed due to local emergency"
  5. Follow guidance provided by first responders

Fire – Inside Building

Should any fire endanger the occupants or staff, it is important to act quickly and decisively to prevent injuries and contain the spread of the fire. In the event of a fire, the following guidelines should be followed:

  • Elevator should not be used
  • Close all doors leading to the fire
  • If area is full of smoke, crawl along the floor close to the walls
  • Before opening a door, check it for heat by placing hand one inch from the door near the top

If a fire of any size is extinguished by onsite personnel, the fire department MUST be called to confirm that the fire is out.

Fire – Outside / Adjacent Building

A fire in an adjoining building or area can threaten the site/building and endanger occupants and staff. Response actions will be determined by the location, size, proximity to building, and the likelihood that it may endanger the health and safety of occupants.

If building is evacuated:

  • Monitor radio for information
  • If fire reached the building, do not return to the building until it has been inspected and determined safe by proper authorities

Earthquake

  1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  2. When shaking stops, remain in place for 20 seconds
  3. Evacuate Building
  4. Shut off gas to building
  5. Prevent re-entry to building
  6. Evaluate the situation
  7. Take additional action as needed

Active Intruder

Initial Actions

  1. If appropriate, initiate lockdown (extreme act of violence)
  2. Call 911 - provide details of situation (i.e. lockdown)
  3. If safe, clear hallways and open areas to direct occupants to safety
  4. Shut off gas to building
  5. Prevent re-entry into building
  6. Evaluate the situation
  7. Take additional action as needed

During Active Intruder Event

  1. Remain calm and quiet in a secured area away from doors and windows
  2. If lockdown is initiated, do not open doors once they are locked
  3. If safe, clear hallways and open areas to direct occupants to safety

After Active Intruder Event

  1. Evacuate to secondary (off-site) location
  2. Provide liaison for family members of injured occupants and staff
  3. Assist law enforcement - provide information about incident
  4. Arrange crisis counseling for occcupants and staff

Bomb Threat

  1. Call 911
  2. Turn off cellular phones and 2-way radios
  3. Do not examine or investigate suspicious objects
  4. If necessary, evacuate to secondary (off-site) location
  5. Account for building occupants
  6. Do not re-enter building until authorities provide clearance

Drop, Cover, Hold

  1. Drop to floor under desks, chairs, or tables
  2. Put your back towards windows
  3. Place head between knees
  4. Hold onto table leg with one hand, cover back of neck with the other
  5. When shaking stops, remain in place for 20 seconds
  6. Evaluate the situation
  7. Take additional action as needed

Lockdown

  1. Initiate lockdown announcement
  2. Move quickly and quietly to closest safe office or classroom
  3. Lock doors and remain in office or classroom
  4. Close blinds or shades in room and turn off lights
  5. Call 911
  6. Remain quiet and calm
  7. Wait for authorized staff or law enforcement to open the door

Aircraft Crash

  1. Call 911
  2. If necessary, evacuate building
  3. If necessary administer first aid and removal of injured from building
  4. Ensure students and staff remain at a safe distance
  5. Account for building occupants
  6. Do not re-enter building until authorities provide clearance

Severe Weather

  1. Monitor weather forecasts and storm information
  2. Report to site by 6 a.m. to check for power outages, flooding, etc.
  3. Notify utility companies of suspected breaks in utilities
  4. Determine if site will be closed or remain open
  5. Contact staff and students if site will be closed
  6. Post site status on website
  7. Take appropriate action to safeguard site property

Windstorm

  1. Evacuate to 1st floor away from spaces bearing full force of wind
  2. Initiate TAKE COVER and stay away from windows
  3. Close all blinds and curtains
  4. Take attendance and report to Site Administrator

Shelter in Place

  1. Initiate shelter in place announcement
  2. If necessary, shut down HVAC
  3. Clear occupants from halls and open areas
  4. Secure offices and classrooms
  5. Report attendance
  6. Utilize information gathering resources
  7. When safe, initiate "All clear" announcement
