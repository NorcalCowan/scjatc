Response Team Lead

The Response Team Lead directs on-scene operations and is responsible for overall management of the incident. Coordinates the actions of the team, interface with First Responders, and provide updates to the ownership/representative.

The Response Team Lead will coordinate the activities of

  • Search and Site Assessment
  • First Aid
  • Student / Staff Liaison
Initial Actions
  1. Assess type and scope of emergency
  2. Determine threat to human life and structures
  3. Activate appropriate emergency action
  4. If evacuation is necessary, verify safety of routes and assembly area
  5. Set up leadership post and obtain personal safety equipment
  6. Organize teams needed for response
During Emergency Event
  1. Determine need and request First Responder assistance
  2. Monitor and assess the situation
  3. Receive updates from other response teams
  4. Coordinate with Student / Staff Liaison to account for students and staff
  5. Reassign and/or release staff as appropriate
  6. Remain in charge of site until redirected or event has ended
After Emergency Event
  1. Receive briefing from public safety agency to obtain “All Clear”
  2. Authorize deactivation of response teams
  3. Provide information for reporting
  4. If necessary, proceed with recovery operations

Search and Site Assessment Team

Assess all facilities on the property(s) for safety and damage, lead any and all search efforts if needed. Review and execute utility shut-offs if needed. Sweep quickly through the buildings to identify location of trapped or injured students and staff. Notify leadership of those who are trapped and or injured.

This is a two-person team with an identified team leader. Take no action that might endanger you. Do not work beyond expertise. Use appropriate safety gear. Size up the situation first before taking any actions.

Equipment and Supplies
  • ID Vest
  • Work and latex gloves
  • First aid backpack
  • Clipboard with job description and map
  • Fire extinguisher if available
  • Backpack with goggles, flashlight, face masks, pry bar, duct tape
  • Hard hat
  • Whistle with master keys on lanyard
  • Caution tape
  • Chalk marker
Initial Actions
  1. If possible, create two teams with two searchers in each team
  2. Put on personal safety gear
  3. Obtain all necessary equipment (i.e. flashlight, marking chalk, first aid backpack)
  4. Contact Leadership before beginning Search and maintain communications
  5. Establish Search Route and communicate it to Leadership
During Search and Assessment
  1. Before entering a space, evaluate if the area can be entered safely
  2. Report structural damage to team leader
  3. Use yellow caution tape to barricade hazardous areas
  4. Do not enter severely damaged areas
  5. One team member remains outside of the area being searched for safety and communication
  6. Building search and sweep process to be completed on each floor:
    • Follow established search to cover all areas
    • If area is safe to enter, search assigned area (following map) using orderly pattern
    • If room is clear, mark the outside of door with an X
    • If room contains injured or dead persons, mark the outside of door with an /
    • Report by phone or radio to the Leadership Post when room or area has cleared
  7. Report gas leaks, fires, or structural damage to Leadership immediately upon discovery
  8. Record exact location of damage and triage on map and report to Leadership

Student / Staff Liaison

Ensure the care and safety of all students on the site except those who are in the First Aid Station. Contact and interface with all family and guests on property to ensure, clear communication, status and evacuations if needed.

Initial Actions
  1. Identify team leader
  2. Put on safety vest or position identifier
  3. Check in with Leadership for situation briefing
  4. If site is evacuating:
    • Verify that the assembly area and routes to it are safe
    • Count students or observe the classrooms as they exit to make sure that all classes evacuate
    • Initiate the set-up of support supplies
During Emergency Event
  1. Monitor the safety and wellbeing of students and staff in Assembly Area
  2. If first aid is needed refer to First Aid Station
  3. When necessary, provide water and food to students and staff
  4. Update records of the number of students and staff in the assembly area
  5. Account for and document all staff and students prior to them leaving the site
  6. Make arrangements to provide shelter for students and staff
After Emergency Event
  1. Return equipment and reusable supplies to building

First Aid Team

Provide first aid support as needed and establish emergency supplies. Establish the First Aid treatment area, triage and emergency medical response/ first aid. Ensure that appropriate actions are taken using basic first aid. Coordinate with the Search and Site Assessment Team and inform Leadership when the situation requires health or medical services that staff cannot provide.

First Aid Area Organization

Locate triage (injury sorting area) at the entry of the First Aid area. This area is for the injured to be quickly evaluated for severity of injury and directed to the appropriate treatment area.

Immediate Care

For people with life and limb threatening injuries that require immediate attention, such as difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, major burns and shock. Locate immediate care in an area out of sight of most students and staff but accessible to emergency vehicles.

Delayed Care

For injured individuals who do not require attention within the first hour. Such people may have lacerations, broken bones or need medication. Locate near the immediate care area, but shield from the sight of the injured in immediate care area.

Initial Actions
  1. Set up First Aid area
  2. Obtain and put on personal safety equipment including vests and non-latex or nitrile gloves
  3. Secure First aid supplies
During Emergency Event
  1. Admit injured students/staff to First Aid area, listing name on master log
  2. Administer appropriate first aid
  3. Keep accurate records of care given
  4. Continue to assess victims at regular intervals
  5. If and when transport is available, do final assessment and document. Keep and file records for reference—do not send with victim. Determine where the injured will be transported to / location.
  6. A copy of the Student’s and Staff Emergency Contact Information must accompany student removed from site to receive advanced medical attention

This action provides for the orderly movement of students and staff along prescribed routes from inside the facility to a designated outside area of safety. Evacuation is considered appropriate for, but is not limited to, the following events:

  • Fire
  • Post-earthquake
  • Post-lockdown
  • Bomb threat
  • Gas leak
Announcement

An announcement should be made over the public address system and by staff in classrooms. This announcement should be similar to the following:

Attention please. We need to institute an EVACUATION of the building. Staff is to take their students to their designated Assembly Area. Students please remain with your Instructor.

Assisting Those With Disabilities

The needs and preferences of non-ambulatory individuals will vary. Those at ground floor locations may be able to exit without help. Others may have minimal ability to move and lifting may be dangerous. Some non-ambulatory people also have respiratory complications. Remove them from smoke and vapors immediately.

Visually-impaired
  • Announce the type of emergency
  • Offer your arm for guidance
  • Tell person where you are going, obstacles you encounter
  • When you reach safety, ask if further help is needed
Hearing-impaired
  • Turn lights on/off to gain person’s attention -OR-
  • Indicate directions with gestures –OR-
  • Write a note with evacuation directions
Crutches, Canes, or Walkers
  • Evacuate these individuals as injured persons
  • Assist and accompany to evacuation site, if possible -OR-
  • Use a sturdy chair (or one with wheels) to move person -OR-
  • Help carry individual to safety
Wheelchairs
  • Give priority assistance to wheelchair users with electrical respirators
  • If wheelchair is too heavy to take downstairs, determine the best carry options
  • Reunite person with the wheelchair as soon as it is safe to do so
  • Use evacuation chair if available and needed

Fire – Inside Building

Should any fire endanger the occupants or staff, it is important to act quickly and decisively to prevent injuries and contain the spread of the fire. In the event of a fire, the following guidelines should be followed:

  • Elevator should not be used
  • Close all doors leading to the fire
  • If area is full of smoke, crawl along the floor close to the walls
  • Before opening a door, check it for heat by placing hand one inch from the door near the top

If a fire of any size is extinguished by onsite personnel, the fire department MUST be called to confirm that the fire is out.

Fire – Outside / Adjacent Building

A fire in an adjoining building or area can threaten the site/building and endanger occupants and staff. Response actions will be determined by the location, size, proximity to building, and the likelihood that it may endanger the health and safety of occupants.

If building is evacuated:

  • Monitor radio for information
  • If fire reached the building, do not return to the building until it has been inspected and determined safe by proper authorities

Hazards and Cautions

The major threat of injury during an earthquake is from falling objects, glass shards and debris. Many injuries are sustained while entering or leaving buildings. Therefore, it is important to quickly move away from windows, free-standing partitions and shelves and take the best available cover under a sturdy desk or table, in a doorway or against an inside wall. All other actions must wait until the shaking stops. If persons are protected from falling objects, the rolling motion of the earth may be frightening but not necessarily dangerous.

  • Elevator should not be used
  • Fire alarms or sprinkler systems may be activated by the shaking
  • Stairways will need to be inspected for damage before they can be used
  • The major shock is usually followed by numerous aftershocks, which may last for weeks
Announcement (if possible)

If possible, an announcement should be made over the public address system and by staff in classrooms. This announcement should be similar to the following:

Attention please. We are experiencing an earthquake. For your protection, follow DROP, COVER and HOLD ON procedures. Get under a table or desk, away from windows and anything that could fall and hurt you. Hold that position until the shaking stops or until you receive further instructions.

Outside Building - Actions and Precautions

If outside the building when an earthquake occurs, follow the steps and precautions below:

During the earthquake
  • Move students away from buildings, trees, overhead wires and poles
  • Make body as small as possible by
    • Dropping to knees
    • Clasp both hands behind neck
    • Bury face in arms, covering ears with forearms
  • Maintain position until shaking stops
After the shaking stops
  • Remain in place for at least 20 seconds
  • Relocate to the proper evacuation assembly area

Evaluate the situation and take further action in accordance with the Evacuation Procedure

During Non-Operational Hours
  • Inspect building to assess damage and determine corrective actions
  • Notify fire department and utility company of suspected breaks in utility lines or pipes
  • If site must be closed, notify staff and students
  • Arrange for alternative learning if necessary

Active Intruder Initiation

Active Intruder is initiated when an intruder within the building poses an immediate threat to the occupants. This action promotes the safety and survival of occupants when under threat by an intruder. The Active Intruder procedure may be initiated by, but not limited to, the following events:

  • Assault on building occupants
  • Use of a weapon to threaten or harm occupants
Active Intruder Execution

During execution of an Active Intruder procedure, the JATC staff is to make efforts to minimize the likelihood of injury to themselves and occupants of the building. The following guidelines should be following during Active Intruder:

Run
  • If occupant can safely do so, run away from the threat to safety
  • Tell others to do the same, but don’t force them to
  • Once safe, call 911 and report any information that may be helpful
Hide
  • If it is not safe to run, hide in the most secure/safe location possible
  • Close, lock, and barricade doors
  • Close blinds and/or shades and turn off lights
  • Identify items that may be used as a weapon if necessary
  • Remain quiet and calm
Fight
  • Never seek out the intruder – fighting the intruder off should only be done if it is the only option
  • Use any item possible as a weapon
  • Work together in numbers if possible

Bomb Threat

  1. Call 911
  2. Turn off cellular phones and 2-way radios
  3. Do not examine or investigate suspicious objects
  4. If necessary, evacuate to secondary (off-site) location
  5. Account for building occupants
  6. Do not re-enter building until authorities provide clearance

Bomb Threat

In the event that the site receives a bomb threat by telephone follow the Bomb Threat Checklist to document information about the threat. Keep the caller on the telephone as long as possible and listen carefully to all information the caller provides. Make a note of any voice characteristics, accents, or background noises and complete the Bomb Threat Report as soon as possible.

Person Receiving Threat by Telephone
  • Listen. Do not interrupt caller
  • Keep the caller on the line with statements such as I am sorry, I did not understand you. What did you say?
  • Alert someone else by prearranged signal to call 911
  • Notify site administrator immediately after completing the call
  • Complete the Bomb Threat Checklist
Person Receiving Threat by Mail
  • Note the manner in which the threat was delivered, where it was found and who found it
  • Limit handling of item by immediately placing it in an envelope so that fingerprints may be detected
  • Notify law enforcement as soon as possible
  • Notify site administrator
  • Caution students against picking up or touching any strange objects or packages

Drop, Cover, Hold

  1. Drop to floor under desks, chairs, or tables
  2. Put your back towards windows
  3. Place head between knees
  4. Hold onto table leg with one hand, cover back of neck with the other
  5. When shaking stops, remain in place for 20 seconds
  6. Evaluate the situation
  7. Take additional action as needed

Lockdown

Initiated to isolate students and staff from danger when there is a crisis on site and movement within the site might put students and staff in jeopardy. Lockdown is used to prevent intruders from entering occupied areas of the building. The concept of Lockdown is no one in, no one out. All exterior doors are locked and students and staff must remain in the classrooms or designated locations at all times. Staff and Instructors are responsible for accounting for occupants and ensuring that no one leaves the safe area. Lockdown is not normally preceded with an announcement. It is appropriate to initiate Lockdown for, but not limited to, the following events:

  • Gunfire
  • Act of extreme violence
Announcement

An announcement may be made over the public address system and by staff in classrooms. This announcement should be similar to the following:

Attention please. LOCKDOWN…LOCKDOWN…LOCKDOWN. Additional information will follow.

Never evacuate from a Lockdown based upon an announcement or fire alarm sounding…this could a distraction used by the intruder!

Safe Locations

If rooms are locked, immediately hide in the closest safe zone: bathroom, janitorial closet, or office area. Lock the door or move furniture or trash can to bar access to the room. Remain quiet until the site leadership or the police provide further instruction.

Aircraft Crash – Near or Into Facility

Emergency protocol will depend on the size of the aircraft, nature of the crash, and proximity to the facility. If it is safe to remain inside the building, all students should be kept in the building under supervision. The crash may also result in an explosion, chemical spill or utility interruption.

Evacuation
  • Move students away from immediate vicinity of the crash
  • Evacuate students from the building using primary and /or alternate fire routes to a safe assembly area away from the crash scene
  • Take class roster/nametags and emergency backpack from storage area located in the break room
  • Check site to assure that all students have evacuated
  • Take attendance at the designated assembly area
  • Report missing students to the administration/designee and emergency response personnel
  • Maintain control of the students a safe distance from the crash site

Severe Weather

  1. Monitor weather forecasts and storm information
  2. Report to site by 6 a.m. to check for power outages, flooding, etc.
  3. Notify utility companies of suspected breaks in utilities
  4. Determine if site will be closed or remain open
  5. Contact staff and students if site will be closed
  6. Post site status on website
  7. Take appropriate action to safeguard site property

Windstorm

  1. Evacuate to 1st floor away from spaces bearing full force of wind
  2. Initiate TAKE COVER and stay away from windows
  3. Close all blinds and curtains
  4. Take attendance and report to Site Administrator

Shelter in Place

A short-term measure implemented when there is a need to isolate students and staff from the outdoor environment to prevent exposure to airborne contaminants. The procedures includes taking actions to prevent exposure to the outside air. Free movement of staff and students within the building is permitted, although one should not leave the room until further instructions are received. It is appropriate to initiate Shelter in Place for, but not limited to, the following events:

  • Gas leaks
  • External chemical release
  • Dirty bombs
  • Hazardous material spills
Announcement

An announcement should be made over the public address system and by staff in classrooms. This announcement should be similar to the following:

Attention please. We have a hazard in the community and are instituting SHELTER IN PLACE procedures. Students and staff should remain inside with windows and doors securely closed and air conditioning units turned off. Those who are outside should immediately move to the protection of an inside room. Do not go outdoors until you receive further instructions.

Give clear instructions, remain calm, and convey reassurance that the situation is under control.

Securing Offices and Classrooms
  • Close and lock doors and windows
  • Shut down office / classroom HVAC
  • Turn off local fans
  • Seal gaps under doors and windows using wet towels or duct tape
  • Seal vents with aluminum foil or plastic wrap
  • Turn off sources of ignition such as pilot lights
